About Chassis and Engine

Hermes Technic & Power Trains

A chassis is the skeletal frame of a vehicle, upon which the body is mounted. It provides a supporting structure for the body, and serves as a mount for the various components and systems of the vehicle, such as the engine, drivetrain, suspension, and brakes. The term "chassis" can also refer to the frame and running gear of a non-vehicular structure, such as a trailer or a train. An engine is a device that converts energy into mechanical work. In a vehicle, the engine is the power source that drives the wheels and propels the vehicle forward. The engine converts the chemical energy stored in fuel into mechanical energy through a series of controlled explosions, or combustions, that take place within the engine. The type of engine used in a vehicle will depend on the intended application of the vehicle. For example, a car may have a gasoline engine, while a tractor may have a diesel engine.
